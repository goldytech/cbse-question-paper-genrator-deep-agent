# CBSE Difficulty Distribution Guidelines

## Standard Difficulty Distribution

All CBSE question papers should follow the 40/40/20 difficulty distribution standard:

- **Easy (40%)**: Questions that require direct formula application, single concept understanding, or straightforward calculations
- **Medium (40%)**: Questions requiring multi-concept integration, problem-solving skills, or 2-3 formula applications
- **Hard (20%)**: Complex analysis questions, multi-step reasoning, or real-world scenario applications

## Difficulty Criteria by Question Type

### MCQ (1 mark)
- **Easy**: Direct recall, single step, 1-2 minutes
- **Medium**: Concept integration, 2-3 steps, 3-4 minutes
- **Hard**: Complex reasoning, 5-6 minutes

### VSA (2 marks)
- **Easy**: Direct calculation, 2-3 steps, 2-3 minutes
- **Medium**: Problem-solving, 3-5 steps, 5-7 minutes
- **Hard**: Application scenarios, 5-7 steps, 8-10 minutes

### SA (3 marks)
- **Easy**: Direct formula application, 3-5 steps, 5-7 minutes
- **Medium**: Multi-concept, 5-8 steps, 8-12 minutes
- **Hard**: Complex analysis, 8+ steps, 12-15 minutes

### LA (5 marks)
- **Easy**: Straightforward problem, 5-7 steps, 10-12 minutes
- **Medium**: Multi-formula, 8-10 steps, 15-20 minutes
- **Hard**: Application/analysis, 12+ steps, 20-25 minutes

### Case Study (4 marks)
- **Easy**: Direct comprehension, 2-3 sub-questions, 8-10 minutes
- **Medium**: Data interpretation, 3-4 sub-questions, 12-15 minutes
- **Hard**: Complex scenario, 4+ sub-questions, 15-20 minutes

## Practical Application

When generating questions:
1. Calculate total questions per section
2. Determine easy/medium/hard counts: 40%, 40%, 20%
3. Apply rounding: prioritize easy when remainders exist
4. Verify difficulty through class/subject-level standards

## Examples

**20 MCQs:**
- Easy: 8 (40%)
- Medium: 8 (40%)
- Hard: 4 (20%)

**15 Questions:**
- Easy: 6 (40%)
- Medium: 6 (40%)
- Hard: 3 (20%)

**11 Questions:**
- Easy: 4 (36%)
- Medium: 5 (45%)
- Hard: 2 (18%)
- Note: Distribution may vary (~10% tolerable)
